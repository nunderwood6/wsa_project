Sen. Steve Daines (R-MT) and Congressman Greg Gianforte (R-MT) claim that 29 Wilderness Study Areas in Montana are unsuitable for wilderness protection. Their consituents disagree. How do we reach consensus about the conservation of wildlands that most people have never visited or even heard of?

Measuring the Value of Wildlands

Wildness

Wildness is calculated using human modification data based on land cover,human population density, roads, and other mapped data on ecological condition.

Darkness

Darkness is calculated using satellite-measured light intensity from the VIIRS nighttime lights data and measures the intactness of the night sky.

Quietness

Quietness is calculated using mapped data of human-generated noise pollution based on field observations and a spatial model using landscape features that influence sound propagation.

Data comparing the threatened lands to 39 National Parks reveals that many of the 29 Wilderness Study Areas have fewer traces of light pollution, noise pollution, and human development than some of our most iconic National Parks. The proposed legislation would open these pristine wilderness areas to the possibility of motorized recreation, road building, and commercial activity such as logging and mining.
