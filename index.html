Wilder than the National Parks

Republican bills threaten nearly one million acres of pristine Montana wilderness. The public lands at risk are wilder, quieter, and darker than the National Parks.

Photos courtesy of Montana Wilderness Association. Photographers: Eric Lane, Zack Porter, Aaron Theisen.

Sen. Steve Daines (R-MT) and Congressman Greg Gianforte (R-MT) are determined to remove wilderness protections for over 800,000 acres of public lands in Montana, despite broad opposition from constituents. Data comparing the threatened lands to 42 National Parks reveals that many of the 29 Wilderness Study Areas have fewer traces of light pollution, noise pollution, and human development than many of our most iconic National Parks. The proposed legislation would open these pristine wilderness areas to the possibility of motorized recreation, road building, and commercial activity such as logging and mining.
